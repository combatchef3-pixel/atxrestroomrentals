Frequently Asked Questions

How many portable restrooms do I need for my event?

The number depends on guest count, duration, and whether food or drinks are served. A common guideline is to increase units for larger crowds.

Can I rent portable toilets for just one day?

Yes. Many providers allow single-day rentals for events, weddings, and short gatherings.

When should I book portable toilets for my event?

Booking at least a few days in advance is ideal, but larger events should reserve units as early as possible to guarantee availability.

How often are portable toilets serviced during a rental?

Servicing frequency varies by event needs and rental duration, but weekly cleaning and restocking is common for longer rentals.

Do ADA compliant units accommodate wheelchairs?

Yes. ADA compliant porta potties have wider entrances and safety features to support wheelchair users.

Hayward, CA Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Hayward (/ˈheɪwərd/) is a city located in Alameda County, California, United States, in the East Bay subregion of the San Francisco Bay Area. With a population of 162,954 as of 2020, Hayward is the sixth largest city in the Bay Area and the third largest in Alameda County. Hayward was ranked as the 34th most populous municipality in California. It is included in the San Francisco–Oakland–San Jose Metropolitan Statistical Area by the US Census. It is located primarily between Castro Valley, San Leandro and Union City, and lies at the eastern terminus of the San Mateo–Hayward Bridge. The city was devastated early in its history by the 1868 Hayward earthquake. From the early 20th century until the beginning of the 1980s, Hayward's economy was dominated by its now defunct food canning and salt production industries.
